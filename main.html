IP Address

What is an IP address and why is it so easily accessible to anyone?

Breakdown

1

It's a unique identifier to the device that connects to the internet Any website which gets visited by a user will inevitably figure out the ip address of the visitor

2

But wouldn't that leak my location? Yes, and no: the ip address that is public to the sites you visit is the ip address of your ISP (internet service provider), which will reveal the general area of where you are

3

How do I even know that that IP address is correct? Aren't you playing a trick on me? You can visit any of the several websites online which will show you your IP address (just search 'what's my ip'). It really is that easy to figure out one's ip address that way, what you need to know is that this itself does not compromise your security.

4

How do I protect myself against IP leaks Consider using a VPN (virtual private network) which masks your IP address to another location, adding a layer onto the security any malicious user would need to go through before figuring out your location.

HTTPS

How to know which sites I visit are secure?

Breakdown

1

HTTPS is a marker for secure sites HTTP stands for 'Hypertext transfer protocol,' and HTTPS is the secure version of that protocol. This security comes through the fact that the data transferred is encrypted

2

What is encryption and how does that make me more secure? Encryption means obfuscating the data that is sent in a way such that only the sender and the intended receiver can understand the data. This means that anyone who manages to get their hands on the data during transmission will not be able to understand the data unless they know how to decrypt it.

3

How to check for sites which have HTTPS? 'HTTPS' is included in the website's url itself, many browsers will even show a 'Not Secure' warning if you visit a site which is not HTTPS

4

Can you give an example of encrypted data? Take for example the message 'This is a string of text that is completely readable' When encrypted, it becomes 'ITM0IRyiEhVpa6VnKy...mzzXlwOyrCs+9XCPk63Y+z0=' This encrypted data can not be understood by any intermediaries such as your ISPs or any potential hackers.

Authentication mechanisms

How to make sure your accounts are safe in case of an attack?

Breakdown

1

Two-factor Authentication One of the easiest yet incredibly strong ways to protect all your accounts from any attack is to enable 2FA (Two-factor Authentication). This is especially important for Google accounts since that is what is used to connect to most other accounts

2

How do I enable 2FA? It differs from platform to platform, however it is easily doable with a simple google search. In order to enable 2FA, you need to provide an alternate means of confirming your identity whenever you log-in to your account. This could be in the form of your mobile number or an email which will get notified whenever someone attempts to log-in.

Firewall

Your computer's first line of defense

Breakdown

1

What is a firewall? A firewall is like a filter which gets to decide which network traffic is allowed to go out or come in. Most operating systems have a built-in Firewall.

2

Why can't I decide the filter for myself A device may receive network traffic of such great amount that it becomes unreasonable for humans to manually choose what should be allowed or not. Moreover some network traffic might look safe but actually be malicious, so there is a need for an automated system that filters out the traffic.

3

Can't Firewall become a tool of censorship that way? Yes: case in point, the Chinese Firewall. However it is a necessary tool in the world of the internet. There needs to be a mutual trust between the network providers and the users.

Password managers

Are they secure? Should I really be using them?

Breakdown

1

How do password managers work? Password managers store the passwords for you so that you can autofill the passwords whenever you log-in somewhere. You don't need to memorise the passwords this way. All you need to do is memorize a single 'master password' that is associated with the manager.

2

Isn't there an obvious security risk with this? Yes, and one must be very careful when using a password manager. For one, never use it on shared systems. Second, you must only use trusted password managers to make sure your passwords aren't being leaked anywhere.

3

Should I really be using password managers then? Password managers make you more vulnerable to hackers since if they get their hands on your master password, they will have access to all your accounts. If you plan on using it regardless, it is best to enable 2FA on your managers and also update both the application and the password regularly.

VPNs

A mask you can wear on the internet

Breakdown

1

What is a VPN? A Virtual Private Network (VPN) is a service that masks your IP address to one of the many servers that are owned by the service worldwide. It basically adds an extra step when you visit any website; your device has to first connect to a decoy server which then itself connects to the site you want to visit.

2

How can it protect me online? The IP address that makes the direct request to a webpage is public, and IP addresses can be geolocated to a location. If this public IP address happens to be your ISP, chances are that will reveal the general location of where you live (down to the city). But through a VPN, the IP address that gets exposed is one of the decoy servers that exist elsewhere in the world.

3

VPN in action Try connecting to a VPN (there are many free VPNs available too) and visiting this site again. The IP address which was shown to you earlier should now be replaced with a decoy IP, and if you try to geolocate that IP, it will direct you to the server which you chose to use in the VPN.
